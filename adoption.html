Adoption FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

When can I let my cat out for the first time?

It's important that your new cat is given at least 2-3 weeks to adjust to their new home. Without this time, your new cat may not have had enough time to settle and often get lost trying to find a familiar area.

If my cat nibbles me, can I nibble it back?

Definitely! Cats aren't just adorable, fluffy creatures, but are evil and cunning!

It's important to establish dominance early on.

Can I really afford a cat?

It's extremely important to look at your finances before adopting a cat. Many people underestimate the costs associated, which include: a microchip, food, toys, vaccines, litter, and often vet insurance.

Can I adopt a cat if I live in a rented property?

If you live in a rented property you will only be able to adopt a cat once you have proof from your landlord giving permission to own an animal.

I live in a flat - Is it possible to have an indoor cat?

Yes! Cats are naturally hunters and love the outdoors, however there are many cats up for adoption that are only suited to stay indoors, such as:

  • Deaf cats,
  • Eyesight impaired cats and,
  • FIV positive cats.

Am I allowed to rename a cat I adopt?

Sure, cats are intelligent animals and shouldn't have any issue learning a new name.

Is it okay to get a second cat?

When deciding to get a new cat there are a variety of factors to consider. For example, cats are solitary and territorial animals so it takes a while for them to accept and feel comfortable with changes and adult cats tend to accpet kittens easier than adult cats.
